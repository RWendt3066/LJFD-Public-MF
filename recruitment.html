Recruitment - Overview


The Lake Johanna Fire Department is an Equal Opportunity Employer. Applications for employment are accepted year round. The hiring process normally starts in the fall based on station staffing needs. Once an application has been received and reviewed, the candidate will be notified to come in for an interview. The candidate will then be sent to a physical agility test and must pass this test to continue on to the psychological evaluation and medical physical. Upon successful completion of these tests, the candidate will be invited to attend an orientation session about the Lake Johanna Fire Department before a job offer is extended.

Pay - Firefighters are paid an hourly rate per call and per drill. They are also paid an hourly wage for working shifts at Station 120 and Station 140 during staffed hours.

Pension - All firefighters who serve 10 years or more are eligible to collect a pension once they reach the age of 50. A minimum of 20 years of service is required to receive the full benefits payout.

If you wish to apply for a position on our department:

  • Download a copy of the LJFD Application
  • Complete all sections of the application
  • Submit the application in person or by mail

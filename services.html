LJFD - Services


Our Department protects the cities of Arden Hills, North Oaks, and Shoreview, with an area encompassing over 30 square miles and a combined population of about 41,000 residents. Services are provided by 4 full time employees and 75 part-time Firefighters, operating out of three fire stations, responding to calls for Fire, EMS, Ice Rescue, Water Rescue, Confined Space Rescue, and Hazardous Materials.

North Suburban Hazardous Materials Team

The North Suburban Hazardous Materials (NSHM) Team is a NIMS Type II joint powers team between the Vadnais Heights, Falcon Heights, Lake Johanna, Saint Anthony, and New Brighton Fire Departments. This Team serves the cities of Vadnais Heights, Falcon Heights, Lauderdale, Shoreview, North Oaks, Arden Hills, Saint Anthony, and New Brighton. The total approximate population served by the NSHM is approximately 100,000 people in suburban Ramsey and Hennepin County.

The NSHM Team primary response area contains the major Interstate Freeways of 694, 35W, and 35E as well as other major state highways, county and municipal roadways. The area also contains multiple universities, several military installations as well as a variety of manufacturing and production facilities.

The NSHM Team is trained and equipped to conduct a variety of activities related to the mitigation of a hazardous materials event, unknown chemical identification, toxic industrial chemical monitoring, radiation monitoring, as well as a variety of chemical/biological weapons detection. Additionally, the NSHM Team is equipped to provide real-time video feeds, scene documentation, plume modeling, onsite weather information, integration with law enforcement operations, and other site and incident support capabilities. The NSHM Team can conduct decontamination efforts for affected personnel including ambulatory and non-ambulatory victims in all weather conditions.
